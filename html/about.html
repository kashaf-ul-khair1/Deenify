The Mission

Why We Built Deenify

We noticed something troubling around us: Muslims were drifting from their practice, not out of lack of faith, but lack of accessible, thoughtful tools. Deenify is our answer — a bridge between Deen and Dunya.

✦
T
Tayyaba Zubaid
Co-Founder & Developer

When our project started, I was very excited. My group mates and I had the same idea. We wanted to create an Islamic app that should help Muslims stay connected to their religion in their busy lives. Since we all agreed, it was easy to start. But as we worked, I realized that converting this idea into a real app is not easy.


We faced many challenges along the way, and we had to work hard to complete the project. One big challenge was staying focused. We had to make sure that everyone did their work on time. Since we were a team, we needed to talk to each other clearly and share the work properly. I had to understand my group mates and make sure we worked well together. I also tried to keep everyone focused and motivated. I learned that teamwork is very important.


Everyone has a different way of working. Sometimes it felt hard to agree on things. I tried to help my team stay focused and interested so that we could complete our tasks step by step. I had to be patient but also firm when needed. I now understand that good teamwork needs patience, focus, and understanding.


Another big part of the project was solving problems. Every step had challenges. We had to improve our idea, decide which features to add, and find the best way to make everything work. Sometimes things did not go as planned and we had to think of new solutions. This helped me improve my critical thinking skills.


One of the hardest challenges was making sure our app would be clear and easy to use. We wanted to include many features but we also had to keep the app simple. This experience helped me understand how important it is to plan carefully before building something. The best part of the project was watching the app take shape and knowing it could help people. This project taught me that working hard and helping each other can make an idea real.


I also learned about time management. I learned how to finish important tasks first and not delay work. In the end, I would conclude my journey as a project that was not just about making an app. It was about learning, teamwork, and personal growth. Now I feel more confident about working with others, fixing problems, and using my time well.

K
Kashaf-ul-khair Khalid
Co-Founder & Designer

When we were assigned the Islamic app project, I wasn't sure how it would turn out. I felt excited about the idea of creating something meaningful, but at the same time I was nervous and confused thinking about the challenges we might face along the way. I felt that as beginners, this task was a bit advanced and complicated. We were quite new to handling a group project as we were not familiar with one another at that time.


Collaborating with teammates to finalize the project's scope appeared quite challenging, as we had differing opinions. Somehow we managed to start the work. We started thinking on some unique idea, and luckily our ideas were congruent. We sensed the same thing that is unfortunately getting common in our society these days: drifting away from our religion. This project was a great opportunity for us to exhibit our vision of bringing change.


I wanted to create something that people could easily understand, with the goal of showing them that our religion is not as difficult to follow as it may seem. Through this app, I aimed to demonstrate how we can seamlessly integrate both our Deen and Dunya, helping users find balance and guidance in every aspect of their lives.


I started searching related apps. I found many features missing. I read the feedback of users — most of them could not get what they were looking for. I started contacting my relatives who had been using such apps for a few years. I asked them which features they wanted in an Islamic app and wrote down their points. It helped us a lot in shaping the features.


Through collaborative discussions, we added and removed several features, and managed to incorporate the opinions of all three of us into the app's structure. We shared our idea with the instructor, who offered valuable guidance that truly broadened our perspectives. His insights sparked new ways of thinking, allowing us to explore innovative solutions and pushing us to approach challenges more creatively.


Balancing this project with my studies was tough. At times, the deadlines felt overwhelming, but breaking the tasks into smaller steps and focusing on one thing at a time really helped. Throughout this project, I gained significant technical and personal growth. This project deepened my connection to Islamic values as I explored ways to present them digitally. I am proud of the outcome and grateful for the support of my instructor and teammates. This experience has strengthened my confidence to handle similar projects in the future.
