Tayyaba Zubaid

When our project started, I was very excited. My group mates and I had the same idea. We wanted to create an Islamic app that should help Muslims to stay connected to their religion in their busy lives. Since we all agreed, it was easy to start. But as we worked, I realized that converting this idea into a real app is not easy. We had faced many challenges along the way, and we had to work hard to complete the project. One big challenge was staying focused. We had to make sure that everyone did their work on time. Since we were a team, we needed to talk to each other clearly and share the work properly. If one person was late, the whole project was affected. I had to understand my group mates and make sure we worked well together. I also tried to keep everyone focused and motivated. I learned that teamwork is very important. Everyone has a different way of working. Sometimes I felt hard to agree on things. I tried to help my team stay focused and interested so that we could complete our tasks step by step. This was not always easy. I had to be patient but, I also had to be firm when needed. I now understand that good teamwork needs patience, focus and understanding. Another big part of the project was solving problems. Every step had challenges. We had to improve our idea and to decide which features to add and find the best way to make everything work. Sometimes things did not go as planned and we had to think of new solutions. I learned how to stay calm and find ways to fix problems. This helped me improve my critical thinking skills. One of the hardest challenges was making sure that our app would clear and easy to use. We wanted to include many features but we also had to keep the app simple. Deciding which features were most important was difficult. We had to test different ideas and choose what worked best. This experience helped me understand that how important it is to plan carefully before building something. The best part of the project was making our app to be able to use. Even though it was difficult, all our hard work paid off. Watching the app take shape and knowing it could help people made me feel happy and proud. This project taught me that working hard and helping each other can make an idea real. I also learned about time management. At first, I thought we had a lot of time. But as the deadline got closer, we realized that we needed to plan and focus. I learned how to finish important tasks first and not delay work. This will help me in future projects at my university and practical life. In the end, I would conclude my journey as a project that was not just about making an app. It was about learning, teamwork and personal growth. Now I feel more confident about working with others, fixing problems and using my time well.

Kashaf-ul-khair Khalid

When we were assigned the Islamic app project, I wasn’t sure how it would turn out. I felt excited about the idea of creating something meaningful, but at the same time, I was nervous and confused thinking about the challenges we might face along the way. I felt that as beginners this task was a bit advance and complicated. we were quite new to handle this task in a group project as we were not familiar with one another that time, it was difficult for me to select members to work on task. I was not aware of the capabilities of other students. Collaborating with teammates to finalize the project’s scope appeared quite challenging, as we had differing opinions. Somehow we managed to start the work. we started thinking on some unique idea, luckily our ideas were congruent. we sensed the same thing that is unfortunately getting common in our society these days, that is drifting away from our religion. And this project was a great opportunity for us to exhibit our vision of bringing change. I wanted to create something that people could easily understand, with the goal of showing them that our religion is not as difficult to follow as it may seem. Through this app, I aimed to demonstrate how we can seamlessly integrate both our Deen and Dunya, helping users find balance and guidance in every aspect of their lives. I started searching related apps. I found many features missing out. I read the feedbacks of users, most of them could not get what they were asked for. I started contacting my relatives who were using such apps from past few years. I told them about my objective. I asked them which features they wanted to have in an Islamic App. I wrote down their points. We also managed to incorporate the opinions and requests of all three of us into the app's structure. As a result, we not only learned new concepts but also saw a significant improvement in our brainstorming skills, taking our teamwork and problem-solving abilities to the next level. Balancing this project with my studies was tough. At times, the deadlines felt overwhelming, but breaking the tasks into smaller steps and focusing on one thing at a time really helped. Regular chats with my teammates also kept us on track and made things easier. Throughout this project, I gained significant technical and personal growth. Collaborating with my teammates enhanced my communication and conflict-resolution skills.
